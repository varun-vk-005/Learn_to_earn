NEET Examination & Preparation

Everything you need to know about medical entrance exams in India

About NEET-UG

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (Undergraduate) is the qualifying entrance exam for MBBS and BDS programs in India. It is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) once a year.

Exam Pattern

  • Total Questions: 200 (180 to be attempted)
  • Subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Biology (Botany & Zoology)
  • Marking Scheme: +4 for correct answer, -1 for incorrect
  • Total Marks: 720
  • Duration: 3 hours 20 minutes

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: Minimum 17 years as on December 31st of year of admission
  • Qualification: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology
  • Minimum Marks: 50% for General, 40% for SC/ST/OBC
  • Attempts: No limit on number of attempts

Important Dates (2023 Cycle)

  • Application Form: March-April
  • Exam Date: First Sunday of May
  • Result Declaration: June
  • Counselling: July-September

Study Strategy & Timeline

2 Years Before NEET

  • Build strong fundamentals in 11th standard subjects
  • Focus on NCERT textbooks thoroughly
  • Join a coaching program or self-study with structure
  • Practice basic problems regularly

1 Year Before NEET

  • Complete 12th standard curriculum by December
  • Take chapter-wise and subject-wise tests
  • Revision of 11th standard topics
  • Start solving previous years' question papers

6 Months Before NEET

  • Complete full syllabus revision once
  • Focus on weaker topics and subjects
  • Take regular full-length mock tests
  • Analyze performance and work on weak areas

3 Months Before NEET

  • Second complete revision of the entire syllabus
  • Take at least 1-2 full mock tests weekly
  • Focus on high-yield topics and frequently asked questions
  • Work on test-taking strategies and time management

1 Month Before NEET

  • Quick revision of complete syllabus
  • Practice 3-4 mock tests weekly
  • Revise formulas, diagrams, chemical equations
  • Maintain healthy routine and mental well-being
