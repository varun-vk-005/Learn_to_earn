Medical Specialties

Explore different domains in the medical field to find your passion

Dermatology

Specializes in diagnosing and treating conditions related to skin, hair, nails, and mucous membranes. Dermatologists handle everything from acne to skin cancer and cosmetic concerns.

Cardiology

Focuses on diagnosing and treating heart disorders including coronary artery disease, heart rhythm disorders, and heart failure. Cardiologists perform various tests and procedures to maintain heart health.

Neurology

Deals with disorders of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves. Neurologists treat conditions like strokes, epilepsy, Alzheimer's, and migraines.

Orthopedics

Focuses on the musculoskeletal system including bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, and muscles. Orthopedic surgeons treat fractures, joint problems, sports injuries, and more.

Ophthalmology

Specializes in eye and vision care, diagnosing and treating eye diseases, performing surgeries, and prescribing corrective lenses. They handle conditions from cataracts to glaucoma.

Pediatrics

Focuses on the health and medical care of infants, children, and adolescents. Pediatricians provide preventive care and treat childhood illnesses and developmental issues.

Psychiatry

Deals with the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of mental disorders. Psychiatrists use various treatments including medications and psychotherapy.

Gastroenterology

Focuses on the digestive system and its disorders. Gastroenterologists diagnose and treat conditions affecting the esophagus, stomach, intestines, liver, and pancreas.

Endocrinology

Specializes in disorders of the endocrine system and its hormones. Endocrinologists treat conditions like diabetes, thyroid disorders, and hormonal imbalances.

Oncology

Focused on the diagnosis and treatment of cancer. Oncologists may specialize in medical, surgical, or radiation oncology to provide comprehensive cancer care.

Trichology

Specializes in the study of the hair and scalp. Trichologists diagnose and treat various hair and scalp disorders, including hair loss, dandruff, and scalp infections.

Urology

Focuses on the urinary tract system and male reproductive organs. Urologists treat conditions like urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and prostate disorders.

Obstetrics & Gynecology

Specializes in women's health, particularly pregnancy, childbirth, and disorders of the reproductive system. OB/GYNs provide care from puberty through menopause.

Pulmonology

Focuses on diseases of the respiratory system. Pulmonologists diagnose and treat conditions affecting the lungs, such as asthma, COPD, and pulmonary fibrosis.

Nephrology

Specializes in kidney care and treating diseases of the kidneys. Nephrologists manage conditions like kidney disease, kidney stones, and hypertension.

Rheumatology

Focuses on diagnosing and treating rheumatic diseases, which affect joints, muscles, and bones. Rheumatologists treat conditions like arthritis and autoimmune disorders.

Hematology

Specializes in the study of blood, blood-forming organs, and blood diseases. Hematologists treat conditions like anemia, hemophilia, and blood cancers.

ENT (Otolaryngology)

Focuses on disorders of the ear, nose, and throat. ENT specialists treat conditions ranging from hearing loss and sinusitis to throat cancer and voice disorders.

Infectious Disease

Specializes in diagnosing and treating infections caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites. These specialists are crucial in managing outbreaks and complex infections.

Plastic Surgery

Focuses on reconstructive and cosmetic procedures to restore function and appearance. Plastic surgeons perform procedures from burn treatment to aesthetic enhancements.

NEET Examination

Everything you need to know about the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test

About NEET

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is a standardized entrance examination conducted for admission to MBBS, BDS, AYUSH, and other medical courses in India. It is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) once a year.

NEET has replaced all other medical entrance examinations in India and is now the single entrance test for admission to medical colleges (except AIIMS and JIPMER until 2019).

Eligibility Criteria

  • Nationality: Indian nationals, NRIs, OCIs, PIOs, and foreign nationals are eligible.
  • Age Limit: Minimum 17 years as of December 31st of the admission year. No upper age limit as per Supreme Court order.
  • Educational Qualification: 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.
  • Minimum Marks Required: 50% aggregate in PCB for general category, 40% for SC/ST/OBC-NCL, and 45% for general-PH candidates.

Exam Pattern

  • Mode of Exam: Offline (Pen and Paper based)
  • Duration: 3 hours (180 minutes)
  • Total Questions: 180 Multiple Choice Questions
  • Subjects: Physics (45 questions), Chemistry (45 questions), Biology (Botany & Zoology - 90 questions)
  • Marking Scheme: +4 marks for each correct answer, -1 mark for each incorrect answer
  • Maximum Marks: 720 marks

Preparation Tips

  • Understand the Syllabus: Thoroughly go through the NEET syllabus and make a study plan accordingly.
  • Strong Foundation: Build a strong foundation in basic concepts of Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
  • NCERT Books: NCERT textbooks are essential for NEET preparation. Master them before moving to reference books.
  • Practice Previous Years' Papers: Solve at least the last 10 years' question papers to understand the exam pattern.
  • Regular Mock Tests: Take regular mock tests to assess your preparation level and improve time management.
  • Revision: Regular revision is crucial. Make short notes for quick revision before the exam.
  • Time Management: Develop effective time management skills to complete the paper within the given time.
